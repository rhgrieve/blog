People talk about taste like it's a passive quality — an eye, a sense, something you either have or you don't. But the more I build, the more I think taste is a *practice*. It's developed by shipping, by seeing what holds up under contact with reality, and by being willing to kill your darlings when they don't.

The feedback loop matters. You can curate mood boards forever and never develop real taste. But ship a few things, live with them, notice what bothers you at 2am — that's where taste comes from.

## Taste as editing

Most of the taste-expressing work happens in the editing phase, not the creation phase. The first draft is exploration. The taste shows up in what you cut, what you tighten, what you leave alone.

This is true for code, for prose, for interfaces, for almost everything.

## The trap

The trap is using "taste" as a reason not to ship. Taste without output is just preference. And preferences are cheap.
