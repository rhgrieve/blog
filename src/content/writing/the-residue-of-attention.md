After a long stretch of focused work, something lingers. It's not the code you wrote or the words you put down. It's a residue — the shape of the problem sitting in your mind like an afterimage.

I've started to think this residue is the actual product of deep work. The artifact is a side effect. The real output is the compressed understanding you carry forward.

## Why this matters

When people talk about "flow state," they usually mean productivity — the feeling of output happening without friction. But I think the more valuable thing is what flow leaves behind. The mental model gets refined. Connections form that wouldn't form in shallow passes.

This is why context switching is so expensive. It's not just the time to re-load the problem. It's that the residue dissipates. You lose the afterimage, and you have to rebuild it from scratch.

## Protecting the residue

I've started structuring my days around this idea. Instead of optimizing for output, I try to protect the conditions that let residue accumulate. Fewer meetings. Longer blocks. Less task-switching.

The output follows, but that's not the point. The point is that the understanding compounds.
